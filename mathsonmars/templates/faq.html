FAQs

Frequently asked questions

We have included answers to commonly asked questions below.

Who

Q: What age range is Maths on Mars for?

Maths on Mars has been developed for primary aged children in grades 1 to 6.

Q: What maths ability is Maths on Mars focused on?

Maths on Mars was developed for students who have a maths learning difficulty or who need help developing a good number sense.

Q: Is Maths on Mars suited to kids with dyscalculia?

We focus on building a good number sense in students. Number deconstruction and reconstruction is an integral part of our learning activities. We also try to provide clear, concise pictorial representations of problems. We believe these aspects of Maths on Mars are helpful to students with dyscalculia.

Why

Q: How is Maths on Mars different to other on-line maths learning sites?

We put a strong emphasis on problem-solving and reasoning and use abundant pictorial representations of problems and solutions. We have hints for each question so that if a student is having difficulty they are able to see a part-solution for the specific problem they are solving. We provide weekly email feedback on progress to parents and teachers. We also provide a reporting dash-board for parents and teachers.

Q: How is Maths on Mars different to correspondance maths centres?

Compared with correspondance centres we have reviewed, we put a stronger emphasis on problem-solving and reasoning and a lesser emphasis on memorization.

When

Q: What is the timeline for completion of Maths on Mars?

We are working in stages and subscribers are able to access lessons as we complete them. We plan to complete all lessons for levels 1 to 6 before the end of 2016. We listen to subscribers and prioritize development according to their requirements.
