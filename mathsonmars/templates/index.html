We focus on maths basics

A solid foundation in Maths will give your primary school child ability, confidence, and a good basis for moving to more advanced maths.

Maths Building Blocks

Guided learning through practice. Your child will start with easy problems and progress only when these skills have been mastered.


Set goals

Regular, short periods of practice help retention, learning and enthusiasm.


Rewards and incentives

Your child will love winning points and medals for regular participation and progress.


Reporting

Analyze your child's progress via a weekly email sent directly to your in-box. The report will provide details on activity and individual progress.


Flexibility

The activities are flexible. You can set a time limit for activities or have no time limit if you feel your child would be distracted by this feature. You are in control.


Distraction free

We keep the activity screen very clean with no animation while the student is working. We don't show any timers so that the student doesn't feel under pressure. We use plain colors and large fonts for activity pages.

About Maths on Mars

Maths on Mars was developed to help children who have not developed a strong number sense.

We created Maths on Mars as we found that other maths sites and correspondence / learning centre based approaches did not fully accommodate the needs of students with maths learning difficulties.

We have focused on providing strategies and guidelines for solving maths problems without relying solely on rote memorization. Students are encouraged to master methodologies before moving on to more advanced concepts. Like a pyramid each level of mathematics requires a broader base of more basic elements. Building a solid foundation at each level is important and we encourage students to work at their own pace.

We believe it is important for teachers and parents to be able to review the progress of students. To do so we have built in a dashboard and optional email reports for teachers and parents to provide detailed feedback on student progress.

The Maths on Mars team is based in Perth, Australia.
